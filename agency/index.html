What it does for your org.

Faster, better onboarding.

  • New hires learn the protocols on ProtoQuiz before they ever step on a truck. They show up knowing the doses, the decision points, and the formulary cold.
  • FTO ride time goes to how to actually run a call — scene management, judgment, the gray-area decisions — instead of teaching the basics that should already be locked in.
  • Time-to-cleared drops. Real labor savings per hire, on top of a sharper finished product.

Lower clinical and legal risk.

  • The more fluent providers are on their protocols, the fewer mistakes they make. Peer-reviewed: structured fluency interventions cut prehospital medication errors 49%.
  • Fewer mistakes mean fewer remediations, fewer adverse outcomes, and fewer lawsuits. EMS negligence cases settle for $825K to $1M+.
  • Live dashboard catches weak topics by name before they become a QA case. Dated, signed-off training records hold up under audit and license-defense complaints.

Higher quality providers.

  • Reps on your actual protocol set, not generic content.
  • Reps happen whenever and wherever. It can be on shift, between calls. You don’t need to add more extracurricular hours, no schedule changes.
  • Crews fluent in their agency’s protocols are better at following them, and also at deviating when needed, with a solid footing.

Clinical Review 5 stages
STAGE 1
TEACHING PEARL
Hyperkalemia ECG progression: peaked T waves → PR prolongation → QRS widening → sine wave → VF/asystole. Any ECG changes with suspected hyperkalemia require immediate calcium.
STAGE 2
SCOPE OF PRACTICE
All levels acquire 12-lead ECG and monitor. ALS: 12-lead interpretation, calcium gluconate 3gm IV and sodium bicarbonate.
STAGE 3
TEACHING PEARL
Calcium gluconate vs calcium chloride: gluconate 3gm preferred in non-arrest hyperkalemia (less caustic to veins). Chloride 1g for cardiac arrest. Flush between calcium and bicarbonate — they precipitate.
STAGE 4
TEACHING PEARL
Albuterol 10-20mg neb (high-dose) lowers serum K by 0.5-1.0 mEq/L. Standard neb dosing also helps. These SHIFT potassium temporarily — only dialysis removes it from the body.
STAGE 5
TEACHING PEARL
After ROSC from hyperkalemic arrest, rebound hyperkalemia is the biggest threat. Calcium and bicarbonate effects are temporary (30-60 min). Patient MUST go to a hospital with emergent dialysis capability.

Common questions.

How does setup work?

Send us your protocol PDF. We build out the question bank, scoped per certification level. Your medical director reviews and signs off — nothing goes live to your crews without that approval. We host on a custom subdomain (e.g. youragency.protoquiz.com), and your crews access by link in any browser. Typical turnaround from PDF to live tenant: about a week.

What if our protocols change mid-pilot?

Send the new PDF. We update the content, your medical director re-signs, your crews see the new questions on their next session.

How much IT lift is this?

None. We host on a custom subdomain. Crews access by link in any browser. No install, no SSO setup required (we support it if you want it).

Six months free. After the free months end, the rate is $50/provider/year, locked for life — not just for the pilot period, permanently for pilot agencies. Open to the next 2 agency partners only.
