According to the protocol, what should be done following hypotension after nitroglycerin administration?
A Additional NTG at lower dose
B Discontinue and transport
C Administer vasopressors
D Give 250 mL NS bolus, reassess, repeat as needed
Correct
For hypotension following nitroglycerin give 250 ml NS bolus, reassess, and repeat bolus as needed. Do not give additional nitroglycerin.
Protocol Reference: p.72 →
denvermetro-protocol.pdf · Page 72 of 193

4015 CHEST PAIN / ACS

NITROGLYCERIN — POST-ADMINISTRATION

Indications

Suspected acute coronary syndrome with systolic BP ≥ 100 mmHg.

If Hypotension Develops

  • Give 250 mL NS bolus, reassess, repeat as needed
  • Do not administer additional nitroglycerin
  • Reassess BP every 2 minutes

Contraindications

  • SBP < 100 mmHg
  • PDE-5 inhibitor use within 24-48 hours
  • Right ventricular infarction
