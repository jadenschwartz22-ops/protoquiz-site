Question 1 of 15
According to the protocol, what should be done following hypotension after nitroglycerin administration?
A Additional NTG at lower dose
B Discontinue and transport
C Administer vasopressors
D Give 250 mL NS bolus, reassess, repeat as needed
Correct
For hypotension following nitroglycerin give 250 ml NS bolus, reassess, and repeat bolus as needed. Do not give additional nitroglycerin.
Protocol Reference: p.72 →
PDF
DMEMSMD · January 2026
3060 Chest Pain · Page 72
Section cited
Give SL nitroglycerin if suspected cardiac chest pain and no contraindication
Cited
For hypotension following nitroglycerin, give 250 ml NS bolus, reassess, and repeat bolus as needed. Do not give additional nitroglycerin.
Nitroglycerin contraindications
  • Hypotension SBP < 100
  • Right ventricular ST-elevation MI (V4R)
  • Recent ED medication use (Viagra, Cialis)
Approved by Denver Metro EMS Medical Directors · Jan 1, 2026

Common questions.

Does our medical director have to approve every question?

Yes. Nothing your crews see goes live without medical director sign-off. You stay in control of the content.

What if our protocols change mid-pilot?

Send the new PDF. We update the content, your medical director re-signs, your crews see the new questions on their next session.

How much IT lift is this?

None. We host on a custom subdomain. Crews access by link in any browser. No install, no SSO setup required (we support it if you want it).

What does the pilot offer actually include?

Six months free. After the free months end, the rate is the standard $100/provider/year, flat, no surprise increase. Open to the next 2 agency partners only. Closes August 1, 2026, or when those 2 slots are taken, whichever comes first. No commitment to continue; cancel anytime.
