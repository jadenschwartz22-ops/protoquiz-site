## 1. One-sentence pitch

ProtoQuiz is a training system that drills EMS providers on the one thing they actually use every shift: their own agency's protocols.

## 2. Core insight (the wedge)

Every EMT and paramedic spends school learning the NREMT — a generic, lowest-common-denominator framework. The day they're hired, that knowledge stops being useful in any specific way. From that point on, the thing they actually need to know is **their local agency's protocols** — set by the medical director, scoped to their certification level, governing every drug they push and every decision they make.

There is no tool built to train on that. There are tools for the NREMT. Tools for trivia. Tools for CE hours. Tools for textbooks. Nothing for the highest-frequency, highest-stakes knowledge an EMS provider uses on the job.

ProtoQuiz is that tool.

## 3. Who it's for

**Buyer:** EMS training officers. (Also: medical directors, QA chiefs, EMS chiefs, anyone responsible for clinical readiness.) They sign the deal, manage the rollout, watch the dashboard.

**Buyer's pain (what makes them suddenly care):**
- A new hire shows up and doesn't know the protocols.
- A QA review surfaces preventable protocol mistakes.
- They themselves are spending unpaid hours building Quizlet decks and Word-doc tests by hand.
- A new medical director rewrites the protocol set and now everyone needs to relearn it.
- They know their crews aren't fluent and there's no good way to fix it.

**User:** EMTs, AEMTs, and paramedics on shift. They open the app on a slow night, between calls, on the couch at home, or during a posted assignment. They want reps that actually map to what their agency expects of them.

**Agency size:** Any. The math works at every scale — small services get a training program they couldn't otherwise build; large services get scoped, dashboard-visible fluency across hundreds of providers.

## 4. The status quo (what buyers do today)

- Paid off-shift trainings — providers come in outside their normal schedule
- Reading the hundred-page protocol PDF (some do, most don't)
- Building their own quiz decks for pharmacology and protocols
- Manually written Word-doc tests, given quarterly
- A general-purpose EMS LMS that handles compliance and CE but isn't built around your specific protocol set
- Nothing

These are reasonable tools — they just weren't built to train on **the actual protocol set**. They train around it. ProtoQuiz fills that specific gap; it doesn't replace what's already working.

## 5. What we are NOT

- **Not NREMT prep.** NREMT prep tools are great at what they do — preparing you to pass the registry. We're for what comes after, in the field.
- **Not a replacement for in-person training.** Hands-on training is irreplaceable. We augment it, freeing FTOs and instructors to focus on judgment instead of teaching the basics.
- **Not a general-purpose LMS.** Existing EMS LMS tools handle compliance, CE tracking, and broad clinical content well — and many agencies already use one. ProtoQuiz is purpose-built for one thing those tools weren't designed to do: drill providers on the agency's own protocols.
- **Not an outsider product.** Built by a working paramedic who's lived this gap firsthand.
- **Not CE hours (yet).** ProtoQuiz isn't a CE provider today — that's a possible future addition, not a current claim. Don't write copy that says or implies CE credit.
- **Not a textbook. Not a study app. Not QA software.**

**Stance toward competitors:** The other tools in this space are good at what they do. We're not trying to displace them — we're filling a specific gap none of them were designed for. Speak about competitors with respect; the wedge is what we *do*, not what they don't.

## 6. Differentiation (the one thing)

**ProtoQuiz trains your providers on your agency's actual protocols.** That's the whole wedge.

Other tools in EMS training are designed to be broad and generic — that's a feature for what they're built for (NREMT prep, compliance tracking, CE delivery). ProtoQuiz is the opposite by design:
- Your PDF, your scope, your formulary, your decision points.
- Page-cited answers — every quiz question references the exact location in your protocol document.
- Scoped per certification level — EMTs see EMT-relevant content, paramedics see paramedic-relevant content.
- Reviewed and signed off by your medical director before crews ever see it.

If a buyer asks "why ProtoQuiz instead of [tool we already use]?" — the answer isn't that the other tool is bad. It's that the other tool was built to do something different. ProtoQuiz fills the specific gap of agency-specific protocol fluency, which nothing else is built to do.

## 7. Supporting value props (in priority order)

1. **Train on what you actually use** (the wedge — see above).
2. **Visibility for command staff.** Admin dashboard shows which protocols and which providers need more reps, by name and by topic. Stop finding out at QA.
3. **Faster onboarding.** New hires can learn the protocol set from day one, on their own time. FTOs spend their time on judgment instead of basics.
4. **Lower legal liability.** Documented, dated proof that your providers were trained and signed off on the current protocol set.
5. **No added training hours.** Providers do reps on shift, on a slow night, or a few minutes a week from anywhere — no schedule changes required.
6. **Fast to stand up.** Send the PDF, tenant goes live in days, medical director reviews and signs off, single login link to the crews. No app store, no IT ticket.

## 8. Proof points

- Built by a working paramedic — not a vendor guessing at the field.
- Pilot pricing: 50% off **for life** for early agency partners. Pilot rate stays locked even after the pilot period ends.
- Page-cited content — every question traceable to the source PDF.
- Medical director approval gate — nothing goes live without sign-off; agency keeps control.
- B2C product (App Store) running on the same engine — not vaporware.

## 8a. Pricing (current model — as of 2026-05-04)

- **Standard:** $100 per provider per month, billed monthly or annually. Flat — no tiers, no setup fees, no add-ons.
- **Pilot rate:** 50% off for life = **$50 per provider per month** for early agency partners. Locked for as long as the agency stays on. (Pilot is the early partnership program — the price isn't a temporary promo; it's permanent for those who sign on now.)

  - Standard: 300 × $100/mo = **$30,000/mo** ($360,000/yr)

## 9. Voice and tone (Hybrid: blunt insight, professional delivery)

We sound like a paramedic who's seen the gap and is doing something about it. Not corporate. Not snarky. Direct, confident, lived-in.

**Hits the right note:**
- "EMS lags behind. Let's fix it."
- "Train your crews on your protocols, based on their scope."
- "Stop finding out at QA."
- "We're medics. We know this is a real gap."

**Wrong note:**
- "Revolutionize your EMS training experience" (corporate hype)
- "The #1 EMS training platform" (unverifiable, generic)
- "Synergize" / "leverage" / "best-in-class" (jargon)
- "We disrupt EMS education" (founder-bro)

**Pronouns:** First-person plural ("we built this") and second-person ("your crews," "your protocols"). Avoid third-person product speak ("ProtoQuiz delivers...").

## 10. Words and phrases — use vs. avoid

**Use:**
- your protocols, your scope, your medical director, your agency
- providers, crews, medics, EMTs, paramedics
- shift, on the job, every shift, every call
- protocol fluency, scoped to their level
- highest-frequency, every single day
- pilot, sign-off, stand up, rollout

**Avoid:**
- learners, students, end-users (we're talking to working professionals)
- platform-as-a-service, SaaS, solution (jargon)
- gamified (sounds toy-like)
- compliance-only framing (that's a tail benefit, not the lead)
- NREMT prep language ("ace your test") — wrong audience
- "run your protocols" — say "use your protocols" instead. "Run" reads ambiguous (run a call? run a script?). Providers *use* protocols.
