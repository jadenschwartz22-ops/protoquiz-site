Data Processing Addendum

Version: 1.0 Effective: 2026-05-20
This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") supplements the Master Services Agreement, Terms of Service, or other written agreement between Teach Me to Live LLC, d/b/a ProtoQuiz ("Provider") and the Customer (the "Principal Agreement"). In the event of conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement regarding data processing, this DPA controls.

1. Definitions

Customer Personal Data
Personal information about Customer's Users and Organization administrators that Provider processes on behalf of Customer in connection with the Platform.
Processing
Any operation performed on Customer Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, and deletion.
Subprocessor
A third-party service provider engaged by Provider to assist in processing Customer Personal Data.
Security Incident
A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Customer Personal Data.
Applicable Data Protection Law
All laws and regulations applicable to Provider's processing of Customer Personal Data, including, where applicable, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the UK Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Roles & Scope

2.1 Roles. For Customer Personal Data, Customer acts as the controller (or data exporter) and Provider acts as the processor (or data importer). Provider processes Customer Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer, as set forth in the Principal Agreement, this DPA, and the Platform's documentation.

2.2 Scope of Processing. Provider processes Customer Personal Data solely to: (a) provide and maintain the Platform; (b) authenticate Users and enforce access controls; (c) generate per-organization analytics, leaderboards, and compliance reports; (d) provide support; (e) prevent abuse and ensure security; and (f) comply with legal obligations.

2.3 Categories of Data Subjects. Users (e.g., EMTs, paramedics, training officers, administrators) of Customer's Organization.

2.4 Categories of Personal Data. Email address, display name, profile photo URL, Google user identifier, certification level, organizational role, quiz attempts, scenario responses, leaderboard entries, and technical data (browser, IP for security and abuse prevention only, session identifier).

2.5 No Sensitive Data. The Platform is not intended to process special categories of data under GDPR Article 9, Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined under HIPAA, or sensitive personal information under CCPA. Customer agrees not to upload such data. Provider may scan or sample Customer-uploaded content for indicators of PHI or other sensitive data and may, without liability, reject the upload, quarantine the file pending review, or remove the file and notify the uploading administrator. Provider's enforcement actions under this Section 2.5 do not waive Customer's warranty against uploading such data.

3. Provider Obligations

Provider will:

  • process Customer Personal Data only on Customer's documented instructions;
  • ensure that personnel authorized to process Customer Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality;
  • implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures (see Section 5);
  • assist Customer in fulfilling Customer's obligation to respond to data-subject requests;
  • assist Customer with security, breach notification, impact-assessment, and consultation obligations;
  • at Customer's choice, delete or return Customer Personal Data after the end of services, subject to legal retention requirements; and
  • make available to Customer all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits as described in Section 7.

4.3 Subprocessor Obligations. Provider remains liable for the acts and omissions of its subprocessors as if Provider had performed them itself. Provider will impose data protection obligations on subprocessors that are no less protective than those in this DPA.

5. Security Measures

Provider implements and maintains technical and organizational security measures including:

  • Encryption. TLS 1.2 or higher for data in transit. AES-256 (or equivalent) for data at rest as provided by Google Cloud and Vercel storage.
  • Access Controls. Role-based access for Provider personnel. Multi-factor authentication for production system access. Quarterly access reviews.
  • Tenant Isolation. Firestore security rules enforce per-organization data isolation. Cross-tenant queries are denied at the database layer.
  • Audit Logging. System events are logged. Administrative actions and security-relevant events are retained for at least ninety (90) days.
  • Network Security. Firewall and DDoS protection at the edge (Cloudflare). Backend services accessible only via authenticated APIs.
  • Vulnerability Management. Dependency security scanning. Critical patches applied within seven (7) days; high-severity patches within thirty (30) days.
  • Backups. Daily automated backups with thirty (30) day retention. Tested restoration at least annually.
  • Personnel. Background checks for personnel with production access (when applicable). Annual security training. Confidentiality obligations in employment and contractor agreements.

6. Security Incident Notification

Provider will notify Customer without undue delay, and in any case within seventy-two (72) hours, after becoming aware of a confirmed Security Incident affecting Customer Personal Data. The notification will, to the extent then known: (a) describe the nature of the Security Incident; (b) identify affected data categories and approximate number of affected data subjects; (c) describe the likely consequences; and (d) describe measures taken or proposed to address the Security Incident.

Provider will reasonably cooperate with Customer in investigating and mitigating any Security Incident.

7. Audits

Provider will make available to Customer, upon reasonable written request and not more than once per twelve (12) month period, information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. Such information may take the form of completed security questionnaires, summaries of third-party audit reports, or policy documents.

Customer may conduct on-site audits only where required by Applicable Data Protection Law and only with at least thirty (30) days written notice, during normal business hours, and subject to Provider's confidentiality and security requirements. Customer bears all costs of audits.

8. Data Subject Requests

If Provider receives a request from a data subject relating to Customer Personal Data, Provider will, unless prohibited by law, promptly forward the request to Customer and not respond directly. Provider will, at Customer's expense, reasonably assist Customer in responding to data-subject access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection requests.

9. International Transfers

Provider primarily processes Customer Personal Data in the United States. Where Customer Personal Data is transferred from a jurisdiction whose laws require additional protections (for example, the EEA, UK, or Switzerland), the Parties will, where required, enter into the appropriate Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent transfer mechanism.

10. Return or Deletion of Data

Upon Customer's written request during the term of the Principal Agreement or within ninety (90) days following termination, Provider will, at Customer's choice, return Customer Personal Data in a commercially reasonable format (CSV or JSON export) or delete it from production systems. Backup copies will be purged according to Provider's standard backup-retention schedule (up to thirty days after deletion).

Provider may retain Customer Personal Data only as required by applicable law or for legitimate business records (such as financial records), in which case Provider will continue to protect such data in accordance with this DPA.

11. HIPAA & PHI

Provider is not a "Business Associate" within the meaning of HIPAA and the Platform is not designed to store, process, or transmit Protected Health Information. Customer warrants that it will not upload PHI to the Platform. Provider is not required to enter into a Business Associate Agreement. If a Customer believes PHI has been uploaded to the Platform inadvertently, Customer should contact legal@protoquiz.com immediately to coordinate removal.

12. General

12.1 Order of Precedence. In the event of conflict, this DPA controls over the Principal Agreement on data-processing matters.

12.2 Liability. Each Party's liability arising under or in connection with this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability set forth in the Principal Agreement.

12.3 Term. This DPA takes effect on the effective date of the Principal Agreement and terminates automatically upon termination or expiration of the Principal Agreement, except that provisions necessary to protect Customer Personal Data already in Provider's possession survive.

12.4 Governing Law. This DPA is governed by the laws specified in the Principal Agreement.
