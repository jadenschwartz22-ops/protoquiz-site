2. Services & Orders

2.1 Platform. Provider will make the ProtoQuiz training platform (the "Platform") available to Customer in accordance with this Agreement and any executed Order Form.

2.2 Order Forms. Specific services, seat counts, fees, and subscription terms are set forth in one or more Order Forms referencing this Agreement. Each Order Form is incorporated by reference. 2.3 Acceptable Use. Use of the Platform is governed by Provider's B2B Terms of Service at protoquiz.com/b2b/terms, which are incorporated by reference.

3. Term

3.1 Initial Term. Each Order Form has an initial term specified therein (the "Initial Term"), typically twelve (12) months from the Order Effective Date.

3.2 Renewals. Each Order Form will automatically renew for successive twelve-month periods (each a "Renewal Term") unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the end of the then-current term. Renewal fees may increase by up to seven percent (7%) per year unless otherwise stated in the Order Form.

3.3 Survival. Sections 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Fees), 7 (Warranties & Disclaimers), 8 (Limitation of Liability), 9 (Indemnification), and 11 (General) survive termination of this Agreement.

4. Customer Data & Security

4.1 Ownership. As between the Parties, Customer owns all data, content, and materials provided by or on behalf of Customer or its Users to the Platform ("Customer Data").

4.2 License. Customer grants Provider a limited, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to host, process, and display Customer Data solely as necessary to provide the Platform during the term of the applicable Order Form.

4.3 Security Program. Provider maintains an information security program with administrative, technical, and physical safeguards including encryption in transit and at rest, tenant isolation, role-based access controls, and security monitoring.

4.4 Data Processing. The Parties' rights and obligations regarding personal data are further described in the Data Processing Addendum at protoquiz.com/legal/dpa.html ("DPA"), which is incorporated by reference.

4.5 Data Export. Upon Customer request during the term of an Order Form or within ninety (90) days of termination, Provider will provide Customer with an export of Customer Data in a commercially reasonable machine-readable format (CSV or JSON).

5. Confidentiality

"Confidential Information" means information disclosed by one Party to the other that is marked as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential. Each Party will: (i) protect the other Party's Confidential Information with at least the same care it uses to protect its own confidential information (and no less than reasonable care); (ii) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except to employees, contractors, and advisors bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as protective; and (iii) use Confidential Information only as necessary to perform under this Agreement.

Confidentiality obligations under this Agreement survive for three (3) years following termination, except that trade secrets remain protected for as long as they retain trade-secret status under applicable law.

6. Fees & Payment

6.1 Fees. Customer will pay the fees specified in the applicable Order Form. Unless otherwise stated, fees are invoiced annually in advance.

6.2 Payment Terms. Invoices are due Net 30 days from the invoice date. Accepted payment methods include ACH bank transfer (preferred), credit card, and wire transfer.

6.3 Late Payments. Past-due amounts accrue interest at the lesser of 1.0% per month or the maximum permitted by law. Provider may suspend service for accounts more than thirty (30) days overdue after providing seven (7) days written notice.

6.4 Taxes. Fees are exclusive of taxes. Customer is responsible for all applicable taxes other than taxes on Provider's net income.

6.5 Non-Refundability. Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, fees are non-refundable.

Provider will use commercially reasonable efforts to make the Platform available with at least 99.5% Monthly Uptime, measured as the percentage of total minutes in a calendar month during which the production Platform is available to authenticated Users, excluding (i) scheduled maintenance announced at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance, (ii) emergency maintenance reasonably required for security, (iii) outages caused by Customer, Customer's network, or third-party services not within Provider's control (e.g., Google Cloud, Vercel, Cloudflare regional outages), and (iv) force majeure events under Section 11.5. If Monthly Uptime falls below 99.5%, Customer's sole and exclusive remedy is a service credit, requested in writing within thirty (30) days of the affected month, equal to: 5% of the monthly-equivalent fee for 99.0%–99.49%; 10% for 95.0%–98.99%; and 25% for below 95.0%. Total credits in any month will not exceed 25% of the monthly-equivalent fee for the affected Order Form. Service credits are the maximum aggregate remedy for service-level failures.

8.1 Exclusion. NEITHER PARTY WILL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, PUNITIVE, OR EXEMPLARY DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, REVENUE, OR DATA, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

8.2 Cap. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT WILL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER UNDER THE APPLICABLE ORDER FORM IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

8.3 Exceptions. The limitations in Section 8.1 and 8.2 do not apply to: (a) breaches of confidentiality, (b) Customer's indemnification obligations under Section 9.1, (c) Customer's payment obligations, or (d) liability that cannot be limited under applicable law. Provider will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Customer against any third-party claim that the Platform, as provided by Provider and used in accordance with this Agreement, infringes a United States patent, copyright, or trademark, except to the extent the claim arises from (i) Customer Data or Customer Content, (ii) use of the Platform in combination with software, data, or services not provided by Provider where the claim would not have arisen but for such combination, or (iii) modifications to the Platform not made by Provider.

If the Platform is, or in Provider's reasonable opinion is likely to become, the subject of an infringement claim, Provider may, at its option and expense: (a) procure for Customer the right to continue using the Platform, (b) modify or replace the Platform so that it is non-infringing while preserving substantially equivalent functionality, or (c) terminate the affected Order Form and refund any prepaid, unearned fees. The indemnified Party will promptly notify the indemnifying Party of any claim, give the indemnifying Party sole control of the defense and settlement (provided that no settlement that imposes any obligation or admission on the indemnified Party may be entered into without the indemnified Party's prior written consent, which will not be unreasonably withheld), and provide reasonable cooperation at the indemnifying Party's expense.

Clinical decisions made by Users in the field are the sole responsibility of the User and Customer. Nothing in this Section 9 obligates Customer to indemnify Provider for Provider's own content; Customer's indemnity under Section 9.1 is limited to the matters expressly stated there.

10. Termination

10.1 For Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any Order Form for material breach by the other Party that is not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice. Provider may immediately suspend access if Provider reasonably determines that continued access poses a security risk.

10.2 Non-Renewal. Either Party may elect non-renewal of an Order Form at the end of the then-current term by providing the written notice required under Section 3.2. There is no mid-term termination for convenience; an Order Form may be terminated mid-term only under Section 10.1 (For Cause) or as expressly permitted in Section 4.2 of the DPA (subprocessor objection).

10.3 Effect. Upon termination, Customer's right to access the Platform ceases. Sections identified as surviving in Section 3.3 continue to apply.
